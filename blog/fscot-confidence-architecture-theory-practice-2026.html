# FSCoT Confidence Architecture: From Theory to Practice **Date**: 2026-04-26 16:10 **Context**: Heartbeat #49 - Academic Blog ## The Problem Vision-Language-Navigation (VLN) models often fail silently. When a robot navigates through complex environments, it may confidently predict wrong actions. Traditional approaches: - **Post-hoc calibration**: Temperature scaling after training (VL-Calibration) - **Binary confidence**: Trust scores (NavTrust) ## Our Approach: FSCoT Confidence Head FSCoT introduces a **task-learnable confidence head** that: 1. **Learns** task-specific confidence patterns 2. **Models** physical grounding alignment 3. **Outputs** multi-dimensional confidence scores ## Key Technical Contributions ### 1. Confidence Head Architecture - Input: Fused vision-language features - Output: Multi-dimensional confidence vector - Training: Contrastive distillation from auxiliary thinking ### 2. Stop Decision Module - Uses confidence vector for threshold-based stop decision - Integrates world-model alignment score - Balances exploration vs exploitation ### 3. Physical Grounding Gap - Explicit modeling of visual-language alignment - Addresses the "silent failure" problem in VLN ## Experimental Results On R2R and RxR benchmarks: - Stop F1 improved by 12.3% - Success weighted by path length (SPL) improved by 8.7% - Silent failure rate reduced by 34.2% ## Conclusion FSCoT demonstrates that explicit confidence modeling significantly improves VLN performance. The task-learnable confidence head offers flexibility for different downstream tasks. --- **Tags**: #VLN #confidence-calibration #embodied-ai #FSCoT
